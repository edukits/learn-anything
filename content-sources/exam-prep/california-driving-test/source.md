# California Driving Test Basics Source

Drivers must understand traffic control signs, right-of-way, safe spacing, and impairment rules.

Core rules for the launch sample:

- A full stop is required at a stop sign and at a red light before turning right when turns are permitted.
- Yield signs mean slow down and give the right-of-way to traffic or pedestrians already in the intersection or crosswalk.
- At a four-way stop, the first vehicle to stop goes first. If vehicles arrive at the same time, the driver on the left yields to the driver on the right.
- A safe following distance gives time to see, decide, and stop. A common minimum is three seconds in good conditions, with more space in rain, fog, or heavy traffic.
- Drivers must yield to pedestrians in marked or unmarked crosswalks.
- For most adult drivers, a blood alcohol concentration of 0.08 percent or higher is illegal. Any amount of impairment can still make driving unsafe.
- When approaching an emergency vehicle stopped with flashing lights, slow down and move over when it is safe.
