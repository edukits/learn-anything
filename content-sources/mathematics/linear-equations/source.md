# Linear Equations Foundations

This candidate topic introduces linear equations in one variable for early algebra learners.

## Source Notes

- An equation states that two expressions are equal.
- Solving an equation means finding the value of the variable that makes the equality true.
- Whatever operation is applied to one side of an equation must also be applied to the other side.
- Inverse operations undo each other: addition and subtraction, multiplication and division.
- A two-step equation is easier to solve by undoing addition or subtraction first, then multiplication or division.
- Like terms should be combined before solving.
- A solution should be checked by substituting it back into the original equation.

## Learner Outcomes

1. Solve one-step equations such as `x + 7 = 12` and `4x = 20`.
2. Solve two-step equations such as `3x + 4 = 19`.
3. Simplify both sides before solving, including combining like terms.
4. Explain the meaning of a solution in context.
